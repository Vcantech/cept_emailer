Dear Aspirant,

As climate volatility increases, technologies mature, and cities grow, architects are being asked to operate with greater responsibility and precision than ever before.

Responding to this challenge, the Faculty of Architecture at CEPT University invites applications from graduates seeking intellectual depth, technical rigour, and meaningful engagement with the realities of practice.

At CEPT, postgraduate education is not an extension of the undergraduate program; it is a space for deliberate specialization.

Studios function as laboratories — spaces for creative exploration of real-world challenges. Small cohorts work with expert tutors to develop arguments, test prototypes, and refine positions through sustained critique. Coursework across the semester, along with summer and winter schools, opens up a wide range of explorations in the built environment. Students have access to workshops, a library with rich resources, archives, material labs, and an extensive network of national and international professionals.

Programs Offered
The faculty offers six postgraduate programs, each anchored in a critical domain shaping the built environment.

Master's in Landscape Architecture (MLA)

Responds to climate change and ecological transformation. MLA prepares professionals to work at the intersection of landscape, ecology, and society – designing across scales from regional systems to urban public spaces.

Explore >

Master's in Interior Design (MID)

A comprehensive and forward-looking program blending interdisciplinary learning, hands-on practice, and strong industry engagement, leading to diverse professional pathways.

Explore >

M.Arch in Housing Design (MHD)

Engaging directly with one of India's most urgent spatial questions – how to build housing that is socially responsive, economically viable, and environmentally responsible. The fully studio-based curriculum allows students to design their own academic pathway.

Explore >

Master's in Conservation and Regeneration (MCR)

Treating conservation as an ecological act of design, this program prepares professionals to reinterpret historic environments responsibly while addressing developmental pressures.

Explore >

Master's in Architectural Tectonics (MAT)

Positioned at the intersection of architecture and technology, MAT examines how detailing, material intelligence, and structural clarity give architectural form to ideas.

Explore >

Master's in Architectural History and Research (MAHR)

Cultivates the ability to think historically and critically about the built environment through research, drawing, archives, and writing.
